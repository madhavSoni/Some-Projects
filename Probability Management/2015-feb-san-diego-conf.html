2015 Probability Management Annual Conference Downloadable Presentations

February 17 and 18, 2015 in San Diego, CA
Harry Markowitz – Nobel Laureate in Economics
Modern Portfolio Theory: the Dawn of Probability Management A first-hand account of the revolution of risk/return analysis.

Shayne Kavanagh – Senior Research Manager, Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA)
“Early movers” in the discipline of probability management within GFOA, and models to help public managers learn probabilistic thinking.

Sam Savage
Curing the Flaw of Averages
New technologies and standards are the first step in addressing systematic errors that occur when single average numbers are substituted for uncertainties.

Phil Fahringer – Lockheed Fellow, Lockheed Martin Corporation
“If your goal is a number, why do you need a distribution?”
Many of our contracts have incentive fees based on achieving a specific value of a performance parameter. Understanding the distributions that drive these parameters helps us understand our chances of attaining various levels of financial return.

Steve Roemerman – CEO, Lone Star Analysis
"Who really owns the data?"
Can corporations claim to “own” and tightly manage their data within a lockbox of proprietary software and standards?

Brian Putt – Decision Analyst, Chevron
Cost and Schedule Risk Analysis
This approach to managing risky projects has been used in the military as well as organizations such as Chevron for decades. SIPmathTM brings it to life!

Sam Savage
The Arithmetic of Uncertainty
Five fundamental concepts underlie calculations with uncertainty and risk.

Doug Hubbard, Hubbard Decision Research
Estimating Probabilities With Data and Without Calibrated expertise.

Hubbard/Savage
The Value of Information (see Hubbard's previous presentation)
What you don’t know can hurt you. What’s it worth to find out?

Pat Delaney, Partner, Davies Consulting LLC
Risk and Regulation
Conversations between regulated industries, regulators, and consumers typically do not explicitly involve uncertainty, which leads to the Flaw of Averages. Today a common, transparent lexicon for communicating uncertainty is elevating the discourse on risk. We explore the evolution of this trend in the power utility industry.
